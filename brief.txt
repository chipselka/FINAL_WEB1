==============================================================
SITE: Liberty Atelier — House of Bold Elegance
==============================================================

ROLE / CONTEXT
--------------
Role: Creative Director for an ultra-luxury, avant-garde fashion house.
Context: "Liberty Atelier" is not a department store. It is a private,
appointment-only atelier where fashion is treated as sculptural art.
The brand is synonymous with power, confidence, and unapologetic femininity.
The aesthetic is a dramatic collision of sharp tailoring and flowing silks.
Target client: CEOs, celebrities, and anyone who commands attention when
they enter a room. The color story is iconic: Deep Scarlet Red and Pristine White.

PURPOSE
-------
To showcase the seasonal collections and house icons in an immersive,
editorial format. The site must feel like flipping through a high-fashion
lookbook. Functionality includes: Collection browsing, Lookbook viewing,
Material/Atelier story, and Private Appointment requests. No "Add to Cart"
nonsense — this is about desire and aspiration.


==============================================================
STEP 2 — BRAND VOICE (for `brand` skill)
==============================================================
Voice archetype: The Confident Curator — Sharp, discerning, and magnetic.
Speaks in declarative statements. Unimpressed by trends, only interested in
timeless construction and attitude. Celebrates the bold.

Tone dimensions:
- Formal ↔ Casual: 2 (Polished but not stuffy)
- Serious ↔ Playful: 4 (A knowing, confident smirk)
- Reserved ↔ Expressive: 5 (Passionate about craftsmanship)

Voice rules:
- NEVER use "discount," "sale," or "affordable."
- Use "House," "Atelier," "Collection," "Silhouette," "Artisanal."
- Describe textures vividly: "liquid silk," "sculptural wool," "butter-soft leather."
- Address the client as "you" or "The Client."

Tagline: "Wear Your Conviction."
Key Phrases: "House Icons," "The Scarlet Edit," "Tailored to Power," "Statement Silhouettes."


==============================================================
STEP 3 — DESIGN SYSTEM (for `ui-ux-pro-max` + `design-system`)
==============================================================
Style keywords: Opulent Minimalism, Editorial, High Contrast, Sharp,
Architectural. Think: A white gallery wall with a single, massive red Rothko.

COLOR SYSTEM (Restricted Palette):
- Base (Background): Pure White (#FFFFFF) or Soft Chalk White (#F8F8F8).
- Surface (Cards/Modals): Crisp White (#FFFFFF) with subtle 1px borders.
- Primary (Accents/CTAs): Deep Scarlet Red (#C80000, #A00000).
- Secondary (Hover/Details): Soft Blush or Rose Quartz (#F5D0D0) — used sparingly.
- Ink (Text): Absolute Black (#000000) or Deep Charcoal (#1A1A1A).

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display (Headlines): "Bodoni Moda" or "Playfair Display" — High contrast, elegant, dramatic.
- Body (Text): "Inter" or "Lato" — Clean, light weight (300/400), highly legible.
- Accent: "Cormorant Garamond" italic for pull quotes.

SPACING & RADII:
- Container max-width: 1440px.
- Card Radius: 0px (Sharp, architectural). Buttons may have 0px or 2px radius.
- Spacing: Generous white space. Section padding: 120px.

VISUAL ACCENTS:
- Sharp 1px black or red borders on interactive elements.
- Hover effects: Text links transition to Scarlet Red. Cards have a subtle scale (1.01).
- Custom cursor: A sharp, thin circle.

STEP 5 — SECTIONS & LAYOUT (for Stitch)
==============================================================
Section order and goal:

1. Hero — Split screen.
   Left: Massive, sharp serif headline: "LIBERTY ATELIER." Subhead: "The Winter Collection."
   Right: Full-bleed editorial image of a model in a flowing red coat against a stark white background.
   CTA: "Explore the Lookbook."

2. House Philosophy — Centered, narrow column of text (max-width 600px).
   Statement about the atelier's commitment to artisanal construction over fast fashion.

3. Icons of the House — Grid of 4 categories (image + label):
   - Sculptural Tailoring (Blazers, Trousers)
   - Liquid Silhouettes (Silk Dresses, Satin Gowns)
   - Outerwear Armor (Coats, Capes, Leather)
   - Footwear & Objects (Heels, Boots, Bags)

4. The Scarlet Edit — Full-width horizontal scroll or carousel.
   Highlighting 5-6 specific pieces that define the season's boldest looks.

5. Material Library — 3 cards focusing on texture:
   - Double-Face Cashmere
   - Duchess Satin
   - Butter-Soft Nappa Leather
   Each card has a macro shot of the fabric.

6. Seasonal Lookbook — Pinterest-style masonry grid. High-fashion editorial shots.
   Minimal text, maximum impact. Images open in a lightbox modal.

7. The Atelier (About) — Split screen.
   Left: Image of hands meticulously sewing or draping fabric.
   Right: Story about the founder, the atelier location, and the bespoke process.

8. Appointment — Centered, bold CTA section.
   "Request a Private Fitting." Brief form (Name, Email, Note).

9. Footer — Minimal. Left: Logo. Right: Social links (Instagram, Vogue, LinkedIn).

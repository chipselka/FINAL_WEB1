House Philosophy

"We do not make clothes.
We construct conviction."

Liberty Atelier was founded on a singular belief: that true elegance is not adorned — it is engineered. Each silhouette is the result of weeks of draping, pinning, and rethinking. Our artisans treat wool as a sculptor treats marble. We do not chase the season. We define it. The Client does not follow fashion. She renders it irrelevant.

Material Library

Crafted from
Excellence.

Textile No. I

Double-Face
Cashmere

Sourced from the high plateaus of Inner Mongolia, our double-face cashmere is brushed cloud-soft on one side, structured on the other. It drapes with authority and whispers against the skin.

Textile No. II

Duchess
Satin

Woven in Lyon, France, from 100% mulberry silk. The weight is sculptural — it holds shape while appearing to liquid pool around the form. A House Icon in its own right.

Textile No. III

Butter-Soft
Nappa Leather

Italian-tanned nappa, drum-dyed in midnight black and deep bordeaux. So supple it folds like fabric, so structured it commands a room. This is leather as armour.

The House

Where Cloth
Becomes
Conviction.

"Every stitch is a decision.
Every seam, a statement."

Founded in the historic 8th arrondissement of Paris, Liberty Atelier began as a singular vision: to create garments that do not merely fit, but transform. Our founder, Margaux Delacroix, trained under the last generation of true couture houses before establishing her own. Today, the Atelier employs fourteen master artisans — each a specialist, from sleeve construction to bespoke embroidery. We produce fewer than 200 pieces per season. Each one is numbered. Each one, a House Icon.
